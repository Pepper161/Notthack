# About

[**The Blockchain for Good Alliance (BGA)**](http://www.chainforgood.org/) is a global nonprofit initiative founded by Bybit, dedicated to advancing blockchain as a tool to address real-world problems. By convening leaders, innovators, and organisations from across the blockchain industry, BGA seeks to drive innovation, collaboration and action toward a more sustainable and equitable world.

# Track Theme

## Blockchain for Real-World Impact

BGA invites builders to develop blockchain-powered solutions addressing real-world challenges across areas such as financial inclusion, digital trust, sustainability, and access to essential services.

This track encourages participants to explore how blockchain technologies can create transparent, resilient, and scalable systems that improve everyday outcomes for communities, businesses, and institutions.

Projects may target challenges in areas including:

- Financial inclusion and access to digital financial services
- Transparent supply chains and sustainability tracking
- Digital identity and credential verification
- Anti-fraud and consumer protection tools
- Public goods and digital public infrastructure
- Climate and environmental monitoring systems

Participants are encouraged to think beyond speculative use cases and instead design practical solutions that demonstrate how blockchain can deliver measurable impact in the real world.

# Track Objectives

The BGA Track aims to:

- Encourage university builders to explore real-world applications of blockchain technology
- Identify promising student founders and technical talent working on impactful ideas
- Promote solutions that enhance transparency, security, and accessibility
- Introduce builders to the growing global ecosystem focused on blockchain for impact

Outstanding and creative projects may also receive follow-up opportunities within the BGA ecosystem.

# What We’re Looking For

Teams should build solutions that demonstrate at least one of the following:

### 🌐 Real-World Problem Solving

A clear problem statement tied to real users, industries, or communities.

### 🌐 Blockchain Utility

A thoughtful use of blockchain technology that improves trust, transparency, or coordination.

### 🌐 Practicality

Solutions that can realistically move toward real-world deployment.

### 🌐 Impact Potential

The ability to improve outcomes for users, communities, or systems.

## ❗Extra Note to Builders❗

BGA conducts multiple hackathons globally, and we receive a high volume of submissions often repeating similar concepts or rely heavily on AI-generated ideas without meaningful development or original thinking.

While AI tools can assist in ideation and development, teams are strongly encouraged to demonstrate genuine creativity, thoughtful design, and a clear understanding of the problem in the world that they are solving. How can blockchain empower a better world? 

For example, project submissions building donation platforms for charities for transparency that require target users e.g. the elderly (who may not be receptive to complex learning curves), those living in poverty (who may lack internet access in the rural parts of the world), etc to use a Metamask login, connect to a particular network, know how to off-ramp their crypto… probably wouldn’t score well on practicality.

Projects that present novel approaches, well-considered user experiences (especially those that serve users who don’t know about blockchain), and original perspectives on real-world challenges will be significantly more competitive during judging.

Submissions that appear to be generic, templated, or minimally adapted concepts may receive lower scores, particularly if they do not clearly demonstrate why the solution is unique or how it meaningfully advances existing ideas. While hackathons may be seen as many as a one-off competition, many meaningful and novel ideas can spin off into breakthrough ideas that change the world. BGA is always on the lookout for such projects.

Teams are encouraged to focus on **building something thoughtful, purposeful, and distinctive**, rather than relying solely on commonly seen hackathon prototypes.

# Bonus Criteria (Expanded)

Projects may receive additional consideration if they demonstrate one or more of the following:

### ✅ Real User Perspective

Solutions designed with a clear understanding of the end user, including how people will realistically interact with the product.

### ✅ Clear Path to Deployment

Projects that outline a credible path toward real-world testing, partnerships, or pilot deployment.

### ✅ Public Good Benefit

Solutions that contribute positively to areas such as financial inclusion, sustainability, digital access, or consumer protection.

### ✅ Responsible Design

Projects that consider ethical implications such as privacy, data protection, and responsible technology use.

### ✅ Scalability Potential

Projects that demonstrate potential to scale across regions, industries, or large user groups.

# Submission Requirements

Teams should prepare:

- A short demo of the working prototype or concept
- A brief explanation of the problem and solution
- An overview of how blockchain is used and why it is necessary
- A clear presentation of the project during judging
